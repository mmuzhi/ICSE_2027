To solve this problem, we need to find the maximum number of buildings that can be chosen such that they all have the same height and are arranged at equal intervals. The solution involves grouping buildings by their heights and then, for each group, determining the longest arithmetic progression of indices where the common difference is consistent.

### Approach
1. **Grouping by Height**: First, we group the indices of buildings by their heights. This helps in processing each distinct height separately.
2. **Finding Arithmetic Progressions**: For each group of indices (sorted by their positions), we look for the longest arithmetic progression. An arithmetic progression here means a sequence of indices where the difference between consecutive indices is constant.
3. **Efficient Search**: For each starting index in the group, we check all possible common differences (d) and count how many subsequent indices (a + d, a + 2d, etc.) are present in the group. The maximum count across all starting indices and common differences gives the solution for that height.
4. **Result Calculation**: The maximum count across all heights is the answer, as it represents the largest number of buildings that meet the given conditions.
