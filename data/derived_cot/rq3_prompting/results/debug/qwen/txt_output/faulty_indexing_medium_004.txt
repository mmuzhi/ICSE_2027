To solve this problem, we need to determine the last moment when a bug is at the center of a rod before it must jump to an ant. The bug is initially at the center of the rod, and it can jump to any ant on either side. The time taken for the bug to jump to an ant is the distance from the center to that ant. The goal is to find the maximum distance from the center to any ant, which corresponds to the last moment the bug stays at the center.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The bug is at the center of the rod (position `n/2`). The left side of the rod (from 0 to `n/2`) contains ants listed in increasing order, and the right side (from `n/2` to `n`) contains ants listed in decreasing order. The solution involves finding the maximum distance from the center to any ant on either side.
2. **Key Insight**: The maximum distance from the center to an ant on the left side is `n/2 - left[0]` (since the leftmost ant is farthest from the center on the left). Similarly, the maximum distance on the right side is `right[0] - n/2` (since the rightmost ant is farthest from the center on the right). The solution is the maximum of these two distances.
3. **Edge Cases**: If either the left or right list is empty, the maximum distance is simply the distance to the other side or zero if both are empty.

### Solution Code
```python
class Solution:
    def getLastMoment(self, n: int, left: List[int], right: List[int]) -> int:
        left_max = n/2 - (left[0] if left else 0)
        right_max = (right[0] if right else 0) - n/2
        return max(left_max, right_max)
```

### Explanation
1. **left_max Calculation**: The leftmost ant's position is `left[0]` (smallest value in the left list). The distance from the center (`n/2`) to this ant is `n/2 - left[0]`. If the left list is empty, this distance is considered zero.
2. **right_max Calculation**: The rightmost ant's position is `right[0]` (largest value in the right list). The distance from the center to this ant is `right[0] - n/2`. If the right list is empty, this distance is considered zero.
3. **Result**: The result is the maximum of `left_max` and `right_max`, representing the farthest distance the bug must travel from the center to reach any ant, hence the last moment the bug stays at the center.

This approach efficiently computes the required result by leveraging the positions of the farthest ants on both sides of the center, ensuring optimal performance with a time complexity of O(1) as it only involves simple arithmetic operations.
