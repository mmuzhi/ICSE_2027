The solution involves a greedy approach where we iterate through the flowerbed and plant flowers wherever possible, ensuring that the current and adjacent positions are empty.

### Approach
1. **Extend the Flowerbed**: To handle edge cases where planting a flower might be possible at the beginning or end of the flowerbed, we extend the flowerbed by adding a zero at the start and end. This allows us to uniformly apply the planting condition without special checks for boundaries.
2. **Iterate and Plant**: For each position in the extended flowerbed (excluding the first and last elements to avoid index issues), check if the current and both adjacent positions are empty (0). If so, plant a flower (set the current position to 1) and decrement the count of flowers needed (`n`).
3. **Check Completion**: After processing all positions, if the count of flowers needed (`n`) is reduced to zero or less, return `True`; otherwise, return `False`.
