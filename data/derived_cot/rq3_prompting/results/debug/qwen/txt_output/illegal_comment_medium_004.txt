### Approach
The efficient approach involves using a monotonic stack to compute the left and right boundaries for each element in the array. The left boundary of an element is the nearest previous element that is less than the current element, and the right boundary is the nearest next element that is less than or equal to the current element. 

1. **Left Boundaries Calculation**: For each element, find the previous element that is smaller than it. This is done by iterating from left to right and maintaining a stack that keeps track of indices in increasing order. For each element, while the top of the stack is greater than or equal to the current element, pop the stack. The top of the stack (if not empty) gives the left boundary.

2. **Right Boundaries Calculation**: For each element, find the next element that is less than or equal to it. This is done by iterating from right to left and maintaining a stack that keeps track of indices in increasing order. For each element, while the top of the stack is greater than the current element, pop the stack. The top of the stack (if not empty) gives the right boundary.

3. **Sum Calculation**: For each element, the number of subarrays where it is the minimum is given by the product of the distances to its left and right boundaries. Multiply this by the element's value and accumulate the result, taking modulo \(10^9 + 7\) to handle large numbers.
