The challenge is to determine the least number of obstacles to remove to form a valid path.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem can be modeled as a weighted graph where each cell is a node, and edges between adjacent cells have weights of 0 (if the cell is empty) or 1 (if the cell contains an obstacle). The goal is to find the shortest path from the start to the destination, where the weight of a path is the sum of the weights of its edges (i.e., the number of obstacles removed).

2. **Algorithm Selection**: Given the grid's structure with weights of either 0 or 1, a 0-1 BFS (Breadth-First Search) is suitable. This algorithm efficiently handles graphs with edge weights of 0 or 1 by using a deque to prioritize processing nodes with lower cumulative weights first. Nodes with a weight of 0 are added to the front of the deque, while nodes with a weight of 1 are added to the back.

3. **Complexity Analysis**: The algorithm processes each cell at most once, leading to a time complexity of O(m*n), where m and n are the grid dimensions. The space complexity is also O(m*n) due to the distance matrix used to keep track of the minimum obstacles encountered to reach each cell.
