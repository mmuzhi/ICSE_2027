### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The task is to identify all starting positions in `s` where a substring can be formed by concatenating all words in `words` in order. The substring must be exactly the words in `words` joined together without any extra characters or changes in order.

2. **Intuition**: The solution involves checking every possible starting index in `s` for a substring of length equal to the total length of all words combined. For each starting index, we verify if the subsequent characters in `s` form the words in `words` in the correct order and with the exact counts.

3. **Algorithm Selection**: We use a nested loop approach:
   - The outer loop iterates over each possible starting index in `s`.
   - The inner loop checks if the substring starting at the current index can be split into the words in `words` in order, each appearing exactly once.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: The algorithm has a time complexity of O(n * m), where `n` is the length of `s` and `m` is the total length of all words combined. This is because for each starting index, we potentially check up to `m` characters.

from collections import Counter
from typing import List

class Solution:

        if not s or not words:
