2. **Form Permutations**: Using the extracted digits, two new integers are formed:
   - The first integer is formed by placing the tens digit in the hundreds place, the units digit in the tens place, and the hundreds digit in the units place.
   - The second integer is formed by placing the units digit in the hundreds place, the hundreds digit in the tens place, and the tens digit in the units place.
3. **Output the Result**: The two newly formed integers are printed in the specified order.

### Solution Code
```python
def main():
    N = int(input().strip())
    a = N // 100
    b = (N // 10) % 10
    c = N % 10
    first_num = b * 100 + c * 10 + a
    second_num = c * 100 + a * 10 + b
    print(f"{first_num} {second_num}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
```

### Explanation
1. **Reading Input**: The input is read as an integer `N`.
2. **Digit Extraction**: 
   - The hundreds digit `a` is obtained by dividing `N` by 100 using integer division.
   - The tens digit `b` is found by first dividing `N` by 10 and then taking the modulus 10.
   - The units digit `c` is obtained by taking `N` modulo 10.
3. **Forming Permutations**:
   - The first permutation (`first_num`) is constructed by placing `b` (tens digit) in the hundreds place, `c` (units digit) in the tens place, and `a` (hundreds digit) in the units place.
   - The second permutation (`second_num`) is constructed by placing `c` (units digit) in the hundreds place, `a` (hundreds digit) in the tens place, and `b` (tens digit) in the units place.
4. **Output**: The two resulting integers are printed in the required order, separated by a space.
