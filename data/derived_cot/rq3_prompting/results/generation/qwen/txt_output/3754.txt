### Approach
The approach involves simulating the journey step by step, utilizing up to `k` changes to maximize the Manhattan distance at any point during the journey. For each step, if changes are available, we consider changing the current move to the direction that yields the highest Manhattan distance at that step. If no changes are left, we proceed with the original move. The key insight is that changing a move to a direction that increases the Manhattan distance at the current step can potentially lead to a higher maximum distance overall, though this greedy approach may not always yield the optimal solution for all cases.
