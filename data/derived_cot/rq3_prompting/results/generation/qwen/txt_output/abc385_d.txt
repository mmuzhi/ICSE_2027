To solve this problem, we need to track Santa Claus's movements on a 2D plane and count the number of distinct houses he passes through or arrives at during his journey. The solution involves efficiently checking for houses along each movement segment without revisiting them multiple times.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: Santa starts at a given point and follows a sequence of moves (left, right, up, down) by specified distances. During each move, he travels in a straight line, and any house lying on this path should be counted only once, regardless of how many times he passes it.

2. **Intuition**: For each move, we need to determine if any houses lie on the path taken. Since moves are either horizontal or vertical, we can leverage spatial indexing to quickly find houses along the path. Specifically:
   - **Horizontal moves (L/R)**: Houses must share the same y-coordinate and lie between the start and end x-coordinates.
   - **Vertical moves (U/D)**: Houses must share the same x-coordinate and lie between the start and end y-coordinates.

3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - **Preprocessing**: Build two dictionaries:
     - `horizontal`: Maps y-coordinates to sorted lists of x-coordinates of houses at that y.
     - `vertical`: Maps x-coordinates to sorted lists of y-coordinates of houses at that x.
   - **Binary Search**: For each move, use binary search on the relevant dictionary to find houses within the move's path.
   - **Tracking Visited Houses**: Maintain a set of visited houses to avoid counting duplicates.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: 
   - **Preprocessing**: O(N log N) for sorting house coordinates, where N is the number of houses.
   - **Processing Moves**: Each move involves a binary search (O(log M)) and possibly iterating over houses in the path. However, each house is checked at most once, leading to an overall O(M + N) complexity, which is efficient given the constraints.
