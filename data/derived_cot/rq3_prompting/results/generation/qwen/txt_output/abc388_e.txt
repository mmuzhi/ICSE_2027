### Approach

2. **Intuition**: The smallest mochi can only be paired with a larger mochi that is at least twice its size. By processing the smallest elements first and matching them with the smallest possible larger elements, we maximize the number of pairs without reusing any mochi.
3. **Algorithm Selection**: We use two pointers, `i` for the smaller mochi and `j` for the larger mochi. For each smaller mochi at `i`, we find the smallest larger mochi at `j` such that `A[j] >= 2 * A[i]`. Once a valid pair is found, both pointers are incremented, and the count of pairs is increased.
4. **Complexity Analysis**: The algorithm processes each element at most once, resulting in a time complexity of O(N), where N is the number of mochi. The space complexity is O(1) as no additional data structures are used.
