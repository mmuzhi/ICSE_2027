   - \( p^2 \times q^2 \), where \( p \) and \( q \) are distinct primes. The number of divisors here is \( (2 + 1) \times (2 + 1) = 9 \).

3. **Case 2 - Handling \( p^2 \times q^2 \)**:
   - We use the Sieve of Eratosthenes to generate all primes up to \( \sqrt{N} \). For each prime \( p \), we find all primes \( q \) (distinct from \( p \)) such that \( p \times q \leq \sqrt{N} \). The number of valid pairs \( (p, q) \) gives the count of numbers of the form \( (p \times q)^2 \leq N \).

4. **Efficiency**: The Sieve of Eratosthenes efficiently generates primes up to \( \sqrt{N} \), and binary search helps quickly count valid pairs for each prime \( p \).
