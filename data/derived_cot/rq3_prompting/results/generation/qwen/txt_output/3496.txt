3. **Feasibility Check**: For each candidate `T`, we calculate the maximum reduction each worker can achieve without exceeding `T`. The sum of these individual maximum reductions must be at least the mountain's height. If it is, then `T` is a feasible solution, and we try a smaller `T`; otherwise, we try a larger `T`.

4. **Binary Search Execution**: The binary search narrows down the minimal `T` by repeatedly checking feasibility and adjusting the search range based on whether the current `T` is feasible or not.
