The operations involve moving all pieces simultaneously towards a chosen center square in each operation.

### Approach

Each operation moves all pieces towards a chosen center, either left or right. The key observation is that the net displacement for each piece depends on the number of operations where the center is to its right (moving it left) or left (moving it right).

2. **Insight**: For each piece in the initial configuration, the final position after all operations must be a target square (where B has '1'). The displacement of each piece is determined by the difference between the number of operations with centers to its right and left. 

3. **Algorithm Selection**: Given the constraints (total sum of N across test cases is ≤ 10^6), we handle each test case individually. 4. **Complexity Analysis**: The brute-force approach for small N involves checking all possible multisets of centers (size k) for each k from 0 to N. The number of multisets for a given k is C(N + k - 1, k), which is manageable for small N (≤10). For larger N, we use a heuristic to quickly determine impossibility.
