To solve this problem, we need to find the maximum difference between the frequency of two distinct characters in a string, where one character has an odd frequency and the other has an even frequency. The difference is calculated as the frequency of the character with an odd frequency minus the frequency of the character with an even frequency.

### Approach
1. **Count Frequencies**: Use a frequency counter to count the occurrences of each character in the string.
2. **Separate Frequencies**: Separate the frequencies into two lists: one for characters with odd frequencies and another for characters with even frequencies.
3. **Find Maximum and Minimum**: From the odd frequencies list, find the maximum value. From the even frequencies list, find the minimum value.
4. **Calculate Difference**: Subtract the minimum even frequency from the maximum odd frequency to get the maximum difference.

### Solution Code
```python
from collections import Counter

class Solution:
    def maxDifference(self, s: str) -> int:
        cnt = Counter(s)
        odd_list = [f for f in cnt.values() if f % 2 == 1]
        even_list = [f for f in cnt.values() if f % 2 == 0]
        max_odd = max(odd_list)
        min_even = min(even_list)
        return max_odd - min_even
```

### Explanation
1. **Frequency Counting**: The `Counter` from the `collections` module is used to count the occurrences of each character in the string. This gives us a dictionary where keys are characters and values are their respective frequencies.
2. **Separating Frequencies**: We then separate these frequencies into two lists: `odd_list` contains frequencies that are odd, and `even_list` contains frequencies that are even.
3. **Finding Extremes**: The maximum value in `odd_list` is the highest odd frequency, and the minimum value in `even_list` is the lowest even frequency.
4. **Calculating Result**: The result is obtained by subtracting the lowest even frequency from the highest odd frequency. This gives the maximum possible difference between an odd and even frequency of two distinct characters in the string.

This approach efficiently narrows down the problem to finding the extremes in the frequency lists, ensuring optimal performance with a time complexity of O(n), where n is the length of the string, due to the single pass through the string for counting frequencies. The space complexity is O(1) since the number of distinct characters is limited to 26 lowercase letters.
