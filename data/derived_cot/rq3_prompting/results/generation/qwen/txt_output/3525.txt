To solve this problem, we need to maximize the total energy boost gained over `n` hours by choosing between two energy drinks, A and B, each providing energy boosts per hour. The key constraint is that if you switch from one drink to the other at any hour, the next hour's energy boost is zero due to a required cleansing period. 

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem involves making a series of decisions (choosing between drink A or B each hour) to maximize the total energy boost, considering the constraint that switching drinks results in zero energy for the next hour. This resembles a dynamic programming problem where the decision at each hour depends on the previous choice.
2. **Dynamic Programming Setup**: We use two variables, `a` and `b`, to keep track of the maximum energy achievable up to the current hour if the last drink consumed was A or B, respectively. 
3. **Recurrence Relation**: For each hour `i`, the maximum energy if drinking A is the maximum of:
   - Continuing with A (previous drink was A) and adding the current A's energy.
   - Switching from B (previous drink was B) and adding zero energy (since switching results in zero energy for the current hour).
   Similarly for drinking B.
4. **Iteration**: We iterate through each hour, updating `a` and `b` based on the recurrence relation. The final result is the maximum value between `a` and `b` after processing all hours.

### Solution Code
```python
class Solution:
    def maxEnergyBoost(self, energyDrinkA: List[int], energyDrinkB: List[int]) -> int:
        n = len(energyDrinkA)
        a = energyDrinkA[0]
        b = energyDrinkB[0]
        
        for i in range(1, n):
            new_a = max(energyDrinkA[i] + a, b)
            new_b = max(energyDrinkB[i] + b, a)
            a, b = new_a, new_b
        
        return max(a, b)
```

### Explanation
1. **Initialization**: We start by initializing two variables, `a` and `b`, with the energy values of the first hour for drinks A and B, respectively.
2. **Iteration**: For each subsequent hour, we calculate the maximum energy achievable by either continuing with the same drink (adding its energy to the previous maximum) or switching from the other drink (resulting in zero energy for the current hour but carrying forward the previous maximum).
3. **Update**: The variables `a` and `b` are updated iteratively to reflect the best choice up to the current hour.
4. **Result**: After processing all hours, the result is the maximum value between `a` and `b`, representing the highest energy boost achievable by optimally choosing between the two drinks while respecting the switching constraint.

This approach efficiently computes the solution in linear time with constant space, making it suitable for large input sizes as specified in the problem constraints.
