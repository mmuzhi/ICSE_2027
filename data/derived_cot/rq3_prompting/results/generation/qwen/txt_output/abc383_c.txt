To solve this problem, we need to determine the number of humidified floor cells in a grid. A cell is considered humidified if it can be reached from at least one humidifier cell ('H') by moving up to D steps without passing through any walls ('#'). The humidifier cells themselves are always considered humidified.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The grid consists of cells that can be walls ('#'), floor cells ('.'), or humidifier cells ('H'). The task is to count all floor cells (both '.' and 'H') that can be reached from any 'H' cell within D moves, moving only through non-wall cells (either '.' or 'H').

2. **Intuition**: We can use a multi-source BFS (Breadth-First Search) starting from all 'H' cells. This approach efficiently explores all cells reachable within D moves from any 'H' cell, marking them with their minimum distance. Cells beyond D moves are pruned to optimize performance.

3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - **Multi-source BFS**: Initialize the BFS queue with all 'H' cells, setting their distances to 0.
   - **Distance Tracking**: For each cell processed, explore its neighbors. If a neighbor is a non-wall cell and hasn't been visited, update its distance and enqueue it if it's within D moves.
   - **Counting Valid Cells**: After BFS completes, count all non-wall cells (both '.' and 'H') whose distances are ≤ D.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: The BFS processes each cell at most once, leading to a time complexity of O(H*W), where H and W are the grid dimensions. The space complexity is also O(H*W) due to the distance matrix.
