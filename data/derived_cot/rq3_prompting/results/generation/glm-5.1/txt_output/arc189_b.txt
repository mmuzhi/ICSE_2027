Looking at this problem, I need to understand what the operation does to the gaps between pieces.

**Key Insight: The operation swaps gaps at positions i and i+2**

If pieces are at positions X₁ < X₂ < X₃ < X₄ and we operate on i=1:
- M = (X₁ + X₄)/2
- X₂ moves to 2M - X₂ = X₁ + X₄ - X₂  
- X₃ moves to 2M - X₃ = X₁ + X₄ - X₃

New positions in order: X₁, X₁+X₄-X₃, X₁+X₄-X₂, X₄

New gaps:
- g₁' = (X₁+X₄-X₃) - X₁ = X₄-X₃ = g₃
- g₂' = (X₁+X₄-X₂) - (X₁+X₄-X₃) = X₃-X₂ = g₂  
- g₃' = X₄ - (X₁+X₄-X₂) = X₂-X₁ = g₁

So the operation swaps g_i and g_{i+2}!

**Consequence:** Gaps at odd positions (1,3,5,...) can be rearranged among themselves, and gaps at even positions (2,4,6,...) can be rearranged among themselves.

**Minimizing the sum:**
Sum = N·X₁ + (N-1)·g₁ + (N-2)·g₂ + ... + 1·g_{N-1}

Since coefficients decrease (N-1, N-2, ..., 1), to minimize the sum we want smaller gaps paired with larger coefficients. So sort each group in increasing order.

- Odd gaps (positions 1,3): [4,3] → sorted: [3,4]
- Even gaps (position 2): [2] → sorted: [2]
