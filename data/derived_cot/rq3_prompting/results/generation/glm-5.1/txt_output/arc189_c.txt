Looking at this problem, I need to find the minimum operations to move all balls to box X.

**Key observations:**
1. For red balls to reach X, they must be in the same P-cycle as X (following P moves red balls)
2. For blue balls to reach X, they must be in the same Q-cycle as X
3. Operating on box i moves red balls to P_i and blue balls to Q_i simultaneously

**Algorithm:**
1. Check feasibility - all red balls must be in P-cycle containing X, all blue balls in Q-cycle containing X
2. Compute S_R (boxes to operate on for red balls) and S_B (boxes for blue balls)
3. Red sequence: boxes in S_R sorted by decreasing distance to X in P-cycle
4. Blue sequence: boxes in S_B sorted by decreasing distance to X in Q-cycle
5. The minimum operations = |S_R| + |S_B| - LCS(red_seq, blue_seq)

The LCS captures shared operations - when both sequences want to operate on the same box, we only need to do it once if the ordering is consistent.

For LCS of two permutations, I use the standard technique: map elements of first sequence to positions, extract positions of common elements from second sequence, then compute LIS.

**Verification with Sample 1:**
- S_R = {1,0,3} (0-indexed), S_B = {4,0}
- Red seq: [1,0,3], Blue seq: [4,0]  
- Common element: 0, LCS = 1
- Answer: 3 + 2 - 1 = 4 ✓
