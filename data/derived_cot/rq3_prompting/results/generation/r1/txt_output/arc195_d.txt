Another perspective: Since swap operations are reversible, the total number of swaps needed to bring a set of elements to the front is additive and depends on the order of deletions. Perhaps we can think of the process as: we will eventually delete all elements. The deletion order (the order in which elements are removed from the front) is a permutation of the elements (since they are removed one by one or in groups, but groups can be considered as multiple elements removed at the same time). However, the constraint that a group must consist of equal values means that if we remove multiple elements at once, they must be equal and they must occupy consecutive positions at the front at that moment.
