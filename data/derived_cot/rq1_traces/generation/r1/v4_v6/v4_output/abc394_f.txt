To solve this problem, we need to find the largest connected subgraph in a given tree that forms an alkane. An alkane is defined as an undirected tree where every vertex has a degree of either 1 or 4, and there is at least one vertex with a degree of 4. 

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The key observation is that an alkane tree must have exactly `c` vertices of degree 4 (carbon atoms) and `2c + 2` vertices of degree 1 (hydrogen atoms), leading to a total of `3c + 2` vertices. The challenge is to find the largest such subgraph in the given tree.
2. **Insight**: The vertices in the alkane subgraph must form a connected subtree where each carbon vertex (degree 4) has at least four neighbors in the original tree. The skeleton of the alkane (formed by carbon vertices) must be connected, and each carbon vertex must have enough neighbors not in the skeleton to serve as hydrogen atoms.
3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - **Step 1**: Compute the degree of each vertex in the original tree.
   - **Step 2**: Identify eligible vertices (those with degree ≥ 4).
   - **Step 3**: Find the largest connected component in the induced subgraph of eligible vertices (using BFS or DFS).
   - **Step 4**: The size of the largest component `c` determines the alkane size as `3c + 2`. If no such component exists, return -1.
