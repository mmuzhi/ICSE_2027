1. **Input Reading**: The input consists of an integer \( N \) (the length of the sequence) and a sequence \( A \) of \( N \) integers.
2. **Sum Calculation**: The sum of all elements in the sequence \( A \) is computed.
3. **Parity Check**: The difference between the total sum and \( N \) is calculated. If this difference is odd, Fennec wins; otherwise, Snuke wins. This decision is based on the observation that the parity of the total moves (after accounting for necessary moves) determines the winner, as the players play optimally to control the game's outcome.
4. **Output**: The result is printed based on the parity check, indicating the winner ("Fennec" or "Snuke").

This approach efficiently leverages the problem's constraints and the observation about move counts and their parity to determine the winner without simulating each move, ensuring optimal performance even for large inputs.
