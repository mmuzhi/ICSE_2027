Alice must remove exactly 10 stones on her first turn. Each subsequent turn, the player must remove exactly 1 fewer stone than the previous player. The player who cannot make their move loses the game.

### Approach
1. **Initialization**: Start with the given number of stones, `n`, and initialize `moves` to 0 and `current` to 10 (the number of stones Alice must remove first).
2. **Simulate the Game**: While there are enough stones to make the current move (`stones >= current`) and the current move requires at least 1 stone (`current > 0`):
   - Subtract the `current` number of stones from the pile.
   - Increment the `moves` count to track the number of successful moves.
   - Decrement `current` by 1 for the next player's turn.
3. **Determine the Winner**: After exiting the loop, the next move (the `moves + 1`-th move) cannot be made. If this move would be Bob's turn (i.e., `moves + 1` is even), Alice wins because Bob cannot make his move. If it would be Alice's turn (i.e., `moves + 1` is odd), Alice loses because she cannot make her move.
