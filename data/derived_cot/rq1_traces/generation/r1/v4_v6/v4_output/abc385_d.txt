There are N houses at points (X_1,Y_1),\ldots,(X_N,Y_N) on a two-dimensional plane.
Initially, Santa Claus is at point (S_x,S_y). He will act according to the sequence (D_1,C_1),\ldots,(D_M,C_M) as follows:

- For i=1,2,\ldots,M in order, he moves as follows:
- Let (x,y) be the point where he currently is.
- If D_i is U, move in a straight line from (x,y) to (x,y+C_i).
- If D_i is D, move in a straight line from (x,y) to (x,y-C_i).
- If D_i is L, move in a straight line from (x,y) to (x-C_i,y).
- If D_i is R, move in a straight line from (x,y) to (x+C_i,y).





Find the point where he is after completing all actions, and the number of distinct houses he passed through or arrived at during his actions. If the same house is passed multiple times, it is only counted once.

Input

The input is given from Standard Input in the following format:
N M S_x S_y
X_1 Y_1
\vdots
X_N Y_N
D_1 C_1
\vdots
D_M C_M

Output

Let (X,Y) be the point where he is after completing all actions, and C be the number of distinct houses passed through or arrived at. Print X,Y,C in this order separated by spaces.

Constraints


- 1 \leq N \leq 2\times 10^5
- 1 \leq M \leq 2\times 10^5
- -10^9 \leq X_i,Y_i \leq 10^9
- The pairs (X_i,Y_i) are distinct.
- -10^9 \leq S_x,S_y \leq 10^9
- There is no house at (S_x,S_y).
- Each D_i is one of U, D, L, R.
- 1 \leq C_i \leq 10^9
- All input numbers are integers.

Sample Output 1

To solve this problem, we need to determine the final position of Santa Claus after a series of moves and count the number of distinct houses he passes through or arrives at during his journey. The houses are points on a 2D plane, and Santa's path consists of axis-aligned segments (either horizontal or vertical) based on the given sequence of moves.

### Approach
1. **Initial Setup**: Read the input values, including the number of houses (N), the number of moves (M), Santa's initial position (S_x, S_y), the coordinates of the houses, and the sequence of moves (direction and magnitude).
2. **Data Structures**: Construct two dictionaries:
   - `unvisited_by_y`: Maps each y-coordinate to a sorted list of x-coordinates of houses at that y.
   - `unvisited_by_x`: Maps each x-coordinate to a sorted list of y-coordinates of houses at that x.
3. **Simulate Moves**: For each move in the sequence:
   - **Horizontal Moves (L or R)**: Determine the segment's y-coordinate and the range of x-coordinates covered. Use binary search to find houses within this range in `unvisited_by_y`. Remove these houses from both dictionaries and update the count of distinct houses.
   - **Vertical Moves (U or D)**: Similarly, determine the segment's x-coordinate and the range of y-coordinates covered. Find houses within this range in `unvisited_by_x`, remove them, and update the count.
4. **Final Position**: After processing all moves, the final position is the last computed point (x, y).
5. **Output**: Print the final position (x, y) and the count of distinct houses passed through or arrived at.
