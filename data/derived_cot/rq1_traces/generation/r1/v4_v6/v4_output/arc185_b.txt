The operation allows us to choose any pair of indices (i, j) where i < j, increment the element at index i by 1, and decrement the element at index j by 1. The solution involves checking constraints derived from the cumulative sums of the sequence and their second differences.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The key observation is that each operation preserves the total sum of the sequence. The operations allow us to redistribute values from higher indices to lower indices, effectively increasing cumulative sums from the left. The goal is to ensure the sequence becomes non-decreasing, which translates to certain conditions on the cumulative sums and their second differences.

2. **Key Insight**: For a sequence to be non-decreasing, the cumulative sums must satisfy convexity conditions. Specifically, the second differences of the cumulative sums must be non-negative. This means for each position, the cumulative sum must be at least twice the previous cumulative sum minus the one before that, adjusted by the original sequence's cumulative sums.

3. **Algorithm Selection**:
   - **For N=2**: Directly check if the first element is less than or equal to the second element. If not, it's impossible to make the sequence non-decreasing.
   - **For N≥3**:
     - Compute the cumulative sums of the original sequence.
     - Compute the second differences (e) of these cumulative sums.
     - Use binary search to find the minimal initial adjustment (x1) such that, after propagating the adjustments through the sequence, the last condition (involving the final cumulative sums) is satisfied. The propagation involves updating each subsequent cumulative sum based on the previous adjustments and the second differences.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: The solution efficiently handles each test case. For sequences of length N=2, the check is O(1). For longer sequences, binary search over possible initial adjustments (31 iterations) combined with a linear pass through the sequence (O(N)) per iteration results in O(31*N) per test case. Given the constraints (sum of N across test cases ≤ 200,000), this approach is feasible.

def main():

    t = int(data[0])
    index = 1

                c[i] = c[i-1] + A[i-1]
            
            e = [0] * (N+1)
            for k in range(1, N):
                e[k] = -(c[k+1] - 2*c[k] + c[k-1])

            low, high = 0, 2000000000
