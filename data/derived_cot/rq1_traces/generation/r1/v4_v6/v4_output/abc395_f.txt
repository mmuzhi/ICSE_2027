=== ANSWER ===
To solve this problem, we need to determine the minimum cost required to make Takahashi's teeth fit together well under specific conditions. The teeth fit together well if there exists a constant integer \( H \) such that for every tooth, the sum of the upper and lower teeth lengths equals \( H \), and consecutive upper teeth lengths differ by at most \( X \). The cost is defined as the total reduction in the lengths of the teeth, where each reduction of 1 unit costs 1 yen.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem requires selecting a value \( H \) such that for each tooth pair, the sum of the adjusted upper and lower teeth lengths equals \( H \). The adjustments can only reduce the lengths, so \( H \) must be less than or equal to the original sum of any tooth pair. Additionally, consecutive adjusted upper teeth lengths must differ by at most \( X \).

2. **Key Insight**: The optimal \( H \) is the maximum value that satisfies two conditions:
   - \( H \leq \min_i(U_i + D_i) \) (since we can only reduce lengths).
   - For every consecutive pair of teeth, \( H \) must satisfy \( H \leq \min(D_{i+1}, D_i + X) + \min(U_{i+1}, U_i + X) \).

3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - Compute the total sum of all original tooth pairs.
   - Find the minimum sum (\( \text{min\_sum} \)) among all tooth pairs, as \( H \) cannot exceed this value.
   - Iterate over each consecutive pair of teeth to compute the value \( C_i = \min(D_{i+1}, D_i + X) + \min(U_{i+1}, U_i + X) \). The minimum \( C_i \) (\( \text{C\_min} \)) across all pairs gives the upper bound for \( H \).
   - The optimal \( H \) is the minimum of \( \text{min\_sum} \) and \( \text{C\_min} \).
   - The total cost is then calculated as the difference between the total original sum and \( N \times H \).

### Solution Code
```python
import sys

def main():
    data = sys.stdin.read().split()
    if not data:
        print(0)
        return
    
    n = int(data[0])
    X = int(data[1])
    U = []
    D = []
    total_sum = 0
    min_sum = 10**18
    index = 2
    for i in range(n):
        u = int(data[index])
        d = int(data[index+1])
        index += 2
        U.append(u)
        D.append(d)
        total_sum += u + d
        min_sum = min(min_sum, u + d)
    
    C_min = 10**18
    for i in range(n - 1):
        A = min(D[i+1], D[i] + X)
        B = min(U[i+1], U[i] + X)
        C_min = min(C_min, A + B)
    
    H = min(min_sum, C_min)
    ans = total_sum - n * H
    print(ans)

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
```

### Explanation
1. **Reading Input**: The input is read and parsed into the number of teeth pairs \( N \), the constraint \( X \), and lists of upper (\( U \)) and lower (\( D \)) teeth lengths.
2. **Total Sum and Minimum Sum Calculation**: The total sum of all original tooth pairs is computed, and the minimum sum (\( \text{min\_sum} \)) among all pairs is identified. This \( \text{min\_sum} \) is the theoretical maximum \( H \) since \( H \) cannot exceed any original tooth pair sum.
3. **Computing \( \text{C\_min} \)**:
   - For each consecutive pair of teeth, compute \( A \) as the minimum of the next lower tooth length and the current lower tooth length plus \( X \).
   - Compute \( B \) as the minimum of the next upper tooth length and the current upper tooth length plus \( X \).
   - The sum \( A + B \) for each pair is tracked to find the minimum value \( \text{C\_min} \).
4. **Determining Optimal \( H \)**:
   - The optimal \( H \) is the minimum of \( \text{min\_sum} \) and \( \text{C\_min} \).
5. **Calculating Cost**: The total cost is the difference between the total original sum and \( N \times H \), which is printed as the result.

This approach efficiently determines the optimal \( H \) by leveraging constraints derived from consecutive tooth pairs and ensures minimal reduction cost while satisfying the problem conditions.
