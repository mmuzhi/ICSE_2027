3 <= n <= 10^5

To solve this problem, we need to maximize the total energy boost obtained by consuming energy drinks over `n` hours. The key constraint is that switching from one drink to the other requires skipping the next hour (i.e., no energy boost during that hour). The solution involves using dynamic programming to efficiently compute the maximum energy boost by considering both continuing with the current drink or switching to the other drink at each hour.

### Approach
1. **Dynamic Programming (DP) Setup**: We use four variables to keep track of the maximum energy boosts achievable:
   - `a1` and `a2` for drink A, representing the maximum boosts starting from the next hour and the hour after next, respectively.
   - `b1` and `b2` for drink B, similarly representing the maximum boosts starting from the next hour and the hour after next.

2. **Backward Iteration**: We process each hour from the last hour to the first. For each hour `i`:
   - **Current Drink A**: The maximum boost is the maximum of either continuing with drink A (adding the boost from drink A at hour `i` and the stored value for the next hour) or switching to drink B (adding the boost from drink A at hour `i` and the stored value for drink B two hours ahead).
   - **Current Drink B**: Similarly, the maximum boost is the maximum of either continuing with drink B or switching to drink A.

3. **Update Variables**: After computing the current values for drinks A and B, we update the variables for the next iteration. Specifically:
   - `a1` and `b1` are updated to the current values of drinks A and B.
   - `a2` and `b2` are updated to the previous values of `a1` and `b1`, respectively.

4. **Final Result**: After processing all hours, the result is the maximum value between `a1` (starting with drink A) and `b1` (starting with drink B).

### Solution Code
```python
class Solution:
    def maxEnergyBoost(self, energyDrinkA: list[int], energyDrinkB: list[int]) -> int:
        a1 = a2 = b1 = b2 = 0
        n = len(energyDrinkA)
        for i in range(n-1, -1, -1):
            currentA = max(energyDrinkA[i] + a1, energyDrinkA[i] + b2)
            currentB = max(energyDrinkB[i] + b1, energyDrinkB[i] + a2)
            a1, a2 = currentA, a1
            b1, b2 = currentB, b1
        return max(a1, b1)
```

### Explanation
- **Initialization**: The variables `a1`, `a2`, `b1`, and `b2` are initialized to zero. These will store the DP states for drinks A and B for the next and subsequent hours.
- **Backward Processing**: The loop iterates from the last hour to the first. For each hour `i`:
  - **Drink A Calculation**: `currentA` is computed as the maximum of either continuing with drink A (adding the current hour's boost to `a1`) or switching to drink B (adding the current hour's boost to `b2`, which represents the boost two hours ahead).
  - **Drink B Calculation**: Similarly, `currentB` is computed as the maximum of continuing with drink B or switching to drink A.
- **Update States**: After computing `currentA` and `currentB`, the variables are updated: `a1` and `b1` are set to the current values, while `a2` and `b2` are set to the previous values of `a1` and `b1` (now representing the next and subsequent hours).
- **Result Extraction**: The final result is the maximum value between `a1` and `b1`, representing the maximum energy boost achievable starting with either drink A or drink B at the first hour.

This approach efficiently computes the solution in O(n) time with O(1) extra space, making it optimal for large input sizes.
