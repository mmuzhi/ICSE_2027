You are given an integer array pizzas of size n, where pizzas[i] represents the weight of the i^th pizza. Every day, you eat exactly 4 pizzas. Due to your incredible metabolism, when you eat pizzas of weights W, X, Y, and Z, where W <= X <= Y <= Z, you gain the weight of only 1 pizza!

On odd-numbered days (1-indexed), you gain a weight of Z.
On even-numbered days, you gain a weight of Y.

Find the maximum total weight you can gain by eating all pizzas optimally.
Note: It is guaranteed that n is a multiple of 4, and each pizza can be eaten only once.
 
Example 1:

Input: pizzas = [1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8]
Output: 14
Explanation:

On day 1, you eat pizzas at indices [1, 2, 4, 7] = [2, 3, 5, 8]. You gain a weight of 8.
On day 2, you eat pizzas at indices [0, 3, 5, 6] = [1, 4, 6, 7]. You gain a weight of 6.

The total weight gained after eating all the pizzas is 8 + 6 = 14.

Example 2:

Input: pizzas = [2,1,1,1,1,1,1,1]
Output: 3
Explanation:

On day 1, you eat pizzas at indices [4, 5, 6, 0] = [1, 1, 1, 2]. You gain a weight of 2.
On day 2, you eat pizzas at indices [1, 2, 3, 7] = [1, 1, 1, 1]. You gain a weight of 1.

The total weight gained after eating all the pizzas is 2 + 1 = 3.

4 <= n == pizzas.length <= 2 * 10^5

To solve this problem, we need to maximize the total weight gained by eating all pizzas optimally over multiple days. Each day, exactly four pizzas are eaten, and the weight gained depends on the day's parity (odd or even). On odd days, the weight gained is the largest pizza in the group, while on even days, it is the second largest. The challenge is to form groups such that the sum of the weights gained over all days is maximized.

### Approach
1. **Sort the Array**: The pizzas are sorted in ascending order to facilitate efficient selection of the largest and smallest pizzas during group formation.
2. **Two Pointers Technique**: Use two pointers, `left` starting at the beginning (smallest pizzas) and `right` starting at the end (largest pizzas) of the sorted array.
3. **Iterate Over Groups**: For each group (day):
   - **Odd Days (1-indexed)**: The weight gained is the largest available pizza (at `right`). Move `right` one step left to exclude this pizza, and move `left` three steps right to exclude the three smallest pizzas (which are not counted in the weight gain).
   - **Even Days**: The weight gained is the second largest available pizza (at `right - 1`). Move `right` two steps left to exclude the two largest pizzas, and move `left` two steps right to exclude the two smallest pizzas.
4. **Sum the Weights**: Accumulate the weights gained from each group to get the total maximum weight.

### Solution Code
```python
class Solution:
    def maxWeight(self, pizzas: List[int]) -> int:
        pizzas.sort()
        n = len(pizzas)
        k = n // 4
        left = 0
        right = n - 1
        ans = 0
        for i in range(k):
            if i % 2 == 0:
                ans += pizzas[right]
                right -= 1
                left += 3
            else:
                ans += pizzas[right - 1]
                right -= 2
                left += 2
        return ans
```

### Explanation
1. **Sorting**: The pizzas are sorted to easily access the smallest and largest pizzas. This ensures that we can efficiently form groups with the desired properties.
2. **Two Pointers**: The `left` pointer starts at the smallest pizza, and the `right` pointer starts at the largest. These pointers help in dynamically selecting pizzas for each group.
3. **Group Formation**:
   - For **odd days**, the largest available pizza (`right`) is selected. The three smallest pizzas (`left` to `left + 2`) are excluded from future selections by moving `left` three steps right. The `right` pointer is decremented to remove the selected pizza.
   - For **even days**, the second largest available pizza (`right - 1`) is selected. The two smallest and two largest pizzas are excluded by moving `left` two steps right and `right` two steps left.
4. **Summation**: The weights from each selected pizza (either the largest or second largest in their group) are summed up to get the total maximum weight gained.
