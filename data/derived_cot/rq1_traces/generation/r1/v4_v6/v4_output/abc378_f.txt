   - The graph must remain simple, meaning the added edge must not already exist in the tree.
   - All vertices in the cycle must have a degree of 3 in the resulting graph. 

2. **Key Insight**:
   - For a vertex in the cycle to have degree 3, the vertices at the endpoints of the cycle (u and v) must have degree 2 in the original tree (since adding an edge increases their degree to 3). 
   - The internal vertices (those between u and v in the tree's path) must have degree 3 in the original tree (since their degree remains unchanged when the edge is added).

3. **Algorithm Selection**:
   - **Subgraph Construction**: Construct a subgraph consisting only of vertices with degree 2 or 3 in the original tree, and edges between these vertices.
   - **Component Analysis**: For each connected component in this subgraph, check if it forms a valid path (chain) where:
     - The component has at least 3 vertices.
     - There are exactly two endpoints (vertices with degree 1 in the subgraph).
     - All internal vertices (those with degree 2 in the subgraph) have degree 3 in the original tree.
     - The endpoints have degree 2 in the original tree.
   - **Counting Valid Chains**: Each valid component (chain) contributes one valid pair (u, v) where adding the edge between u and v satisfies all conditions.
