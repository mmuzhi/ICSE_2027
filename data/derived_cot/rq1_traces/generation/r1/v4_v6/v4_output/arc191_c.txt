2. **Key Insights**:
   - **For N = 1**: Any A and M = 1 will satisfy the condition since \(A^1 - 1\) is always divisible by 1.
   - **For N > 1**: We use two main methods:
     - **Multiple Prime Factors Method**: Factorize N into its prime factors. For each prime factor \(p_i^{e_i}\), find a prime \(q_i = k \cdot p_i^{e_i} + 1\) such that 2 has order \(p_i^{e_i}\) modulo \(q_i\). The modulus M is the product of these primes, and A is set to 2. The multiplicative order of 2 modulo M will be the least common multiple (LCM) of the orders modulo each \(q_i\), which is N.
     - **Single Prime Method**: If the multiple prime factors method fails, find a prime \(q = k \cdot N + 1\) such that 2 has order N modulo q. Here, M is q, and A is 2.

3. **Algorithm Selection**:
   - **Factorization**: Factorize N into its prime factors using a precomputed list of primes up to 100,000.
   - **Prime Generation**: For each prime factor, generate primes of the form \(k \cdot \text{base} + 1\) and check conditions using modular exponentiation.
   - **Miller-Rabin Primality Test**: Efficiently test the generated numbers for primality.
   - **Fallback**: If the primary methods fail, try alternative bases or fallback solutions.

4. **Complexity Analysis**:
