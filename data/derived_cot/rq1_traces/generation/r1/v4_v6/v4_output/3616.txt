Reverse your movement direction (left becomes right and vice versa).

The process involves starting at a zero cell, moving in a chosen direction, and handling encounters with non-zero cells by decrementing them and reversing the direction. The solution involves simulating the process for each possible starting zero and direction, checking if all elements become zero at the end.

### Approach
1. **Initialization**: For each zero in the array, consider both left and right directions as starting points.
2. **Simulation**: For each starting position and direction, simulate the process:
   - **Zero Handling**: If the current cell is zero, jump to the next non-zero cell in the current direction (if any). If no such cell exists, the robot moves out of bounds, ending the process.
   - **Non-zero Handling**: If the current cell is non-zero, decrement its value. If it becomes zero, remove it from the set of non-zero cells. Reverse the direction and move one step in the new direction.
3. **Termination Check**: The process ends when the robot moves out of bounds. After termination, check if all elements in the array are zero.
4. **Validation**: Count the starting positions and directions that result in all elements being zero after the process.
