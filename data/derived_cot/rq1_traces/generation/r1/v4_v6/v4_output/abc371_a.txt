   - For brother A to be the middle, the comparisons with B and C (S_AB and S_AC) must differ (one '<' and one '>').
   - For brother B to be the middle, the comparisons with A and C (S_AB and S_BC) must be the same (both '<' or both '>').
   - For brother C to be the middle, the comparisons with A and B (S_AC and S_BC) must differ (one '<' and one '>').
3. **Algorithm Selection**: The solution checks the given conditions for each brother in sequence. If brother A meets his condition, he is the middle. If not, brother B is checked. If brother B doesn't meet his condition, brother C must be the middle by default.

def main():
    s_ab, s_ac, s_bc = input().split()

if __name__ == "__main__":
