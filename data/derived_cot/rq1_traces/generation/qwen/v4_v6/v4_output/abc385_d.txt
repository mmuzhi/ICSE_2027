There are N houses at points (X_1,Y_1),\ldots,(X_N,Y_N) on a two-dimensional plane.
Initially, Santa Claus is at point (S_x,S_y). He will act according to the sequence (D_1,C_1),\ldots,(D_M,C_M) as follows:

- For i=1,2,\ldots,M in order, he moves as follows:
- Let (x,y) be the point where he currently is.
- If D_i is U, move in a straight line from (x,y) to (x,y+C_i).
- If D_i is D, move in a straight line from (x,y) to (x,y-C_i).
- If D_i is L, move in a straight line from (x,y) to (x-C_i,y).
- If D_i is R, move in a straight line from (x,y) to (x+C_i,y).





Find the point where he is after completing all actions, and the number of distinct houses he passed through or arrived at during his actions. If the same house is passed multiple times, it is only counted once.

N M S_x S_y
X_1 Y_1
\vdots

D_1 C_1
\vdots
D_M C_M

Output

Let (X,Y) be the point where he is after completing all actions, and C be the number of distinct houses passed through or arrived at. Print X,Y,C in this order separated by spaces.

Constraints


- 1 \leq N \leq 2\times 10^5
- 1 \leq M \leq 2\times 10^5
- -10^9 \leq X_i,Y_i \leq 10^9
- The pairs (X_i,Y_i) are distinct.
- -10^9 \leq S_x,S_y \leq 10^9
- There is no house at (S_x,S_y).
- Each D_i is one of U, D, L, R.
- 1 \leq C_i \leq 10^9
- All input numbers are integers.

To solve this problem, we need to determine the final position of Santa Claus after following a sequence of moves on a 2D plane and count the number of distinct houses he passes through or arrives at during his journey. The solution involves processing each move, tracking Santa's position, and efficiently checking for houses along his path using spatial data structures.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: Santa starts at a given point and follows a sequence of moves (left, right, up, down) with specified distances. During each move, he moves in a straight line horizontally or vertically. The goal is to find his final position and count how many distinct houses from a given list he passes through or arrives at during these moves.

2. **Intuition**: For each move, we need to check if any house lies on the path taken during that move. To efficiently handle this, we preprocess the houses into two dictionaries:
   - **Horizontal Dictionary**: Maps y-coordinates to sorted lists of x-coordinates of houses at that y-coordinate.
   - **Vertical Dictionary**: Maps x-coordinates to sorted lists of y-coordinates of houses at that x-coordinate.

3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - **Binary Search**: For each move, use binary search on the relevant dictionary (horizontal or vertical) to quickly find houses within the segment of the move.
   - **Set for Tracking Visited Houses**: Maintain a set to record houses once they are encountered to avoid counting duplicates.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: 
   - **Preprocessing**: Sorting the house coordinates into dictionaries takes O(N log N) time.
   - **Processing Moves**: Each move involves a binary search and potentially iterating over a subset of houses. The total number of house checks across all moves is O(N + M log N), which is efficient given the constraints.
