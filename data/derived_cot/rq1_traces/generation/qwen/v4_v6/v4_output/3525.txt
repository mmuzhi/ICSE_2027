=== ANSWER ===

To solve this problem, we need to maximize the total energy boost obtained by drinking one of two energy drinks per hour, considering that switching between the drinks results in a cleanse hour where no energy is gained. The solution involves dynamic programming to efficiently track the maximum energy achievable by ending each hour with either drink A or drink B.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem requires selecting one drink per hour from two options (A or B) such that switching drinks results in a loss of that hour's energy. The goal is to maximize the total energy over all hours.
2. **Dynamic Programming Setup**: We use two variables, `dp0` and `dp1`, to represent the maximum energy achievable up to the current hour if the last drink consumed was A or B, respectively.
3. **Initialization**: For the first hour, initialize `dp0` with the energy of drink A and `dp1` with the energy of drink B.
4. **Iteration**: For each subsequent hour, update `dp0` and `dp1`:
   - `dp0` is updated by either continuing with drink A (adding its energy to the previous `dp0`) or switching from drink B (carrying forward the previous `dp1` without adding any energy for the switch).
   - Similarly, `dp1` is updated by either continuing with drink B (adding its energy to the previous `dp1`) or switching from drink A (carrying forward the previous `dp0` without adding any energy).
5. **Result Extraction**: After processing all hours, the result is the maximum value between `dp0` and `dp1`.

### Solution Code
```python
class Solution:
    def maxEnergyBoost(self, energyDrinkA: List[int], energyDrinkB: List[int]) -> int:
        n = len(energyDrinkA)
        dp0, dp1 = energyDrinkA[0], energyDrinkB[0]
        for i in range(1, n):
            new_dp0 = max(dp0 + energyDrinkA[i], dp1)
            new_dp1 = max(dp1 + energyDrinkB[i], dp0)
            dp0, dp1 = new_dp0, new_dp1
        return max(dp0, dp1)
```

### Explanation
- **Initialization**: The variables `dp0` and `dp1` start with the energy values of the first hour for drinks A and B, respectively.
- **Iteration**: For each subsequent hour, the algorithm calculates the maximum energy achievable by either continuing with the same drink (adding its current hour's energy) or switching from the other drink (carrying forward the previous maximum energy without adding any for the switch hour).
- **Result**: After processing all hours, the solution returns the higher value between `dp0` and `dp1`, representing the maximum energy boost achievable by optimally choosing drinks each hour while accounting for any necessary cleanses during switches.

This approach efficiently computes the solution in O(n) time with O(1) space complexity, making it suitable for large input sizes as specified in the problem constraints.
