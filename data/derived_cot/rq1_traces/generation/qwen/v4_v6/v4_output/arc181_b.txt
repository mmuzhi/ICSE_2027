To solve this problem, we need to determine if there exists a string \( T \) such that the strings generated by the functions \( f(S, T, X) \) and \( f(S, T, Y) \) are equal. The function \( f(S, T, X) \) constructs a string by appending \( S \) for each '0' in \( X \) and \( T \) for each '1' in \( X \). The solution involves checking specific conditions derived from the counts of '0's and '1's in \( X \) and \( Y \), and the length of \( S \), to efficiently determine the existence of such a \( T \).

### Approach
1. **Initial Checks**:
   - **Case 1**: If the counts of '0's and '1's in \( X \) and \( Y \) are identical, then any \( T \) (including the empty string) will satisfy the condition, so the answer is "Yes".
   - **Case 2**: If the counts of '1's in \( X \) and \( Y \) are the same but the counts of '0's differ, no solution exists, so the answer is "No".

2. **Equation Check**:
   - The lengths of the strings \( f(S, T, X) \) and \( f(S, T, Y) \) must be equal. This leads to the equation \((a - c) \times \text{len}(S) = (d - b) \times \text{len}(T)\), where \( a, b, c, d \) are the counts of '0's and '1's in \( X \) and \( Y \) respectively.
   - If the equation does not hold (i.e., the right-hand side is not an integer or negative), the answer is "No".

3. **Special Cases**:
   - If the length of \( T \) (denoted \( n0 \)) is zero, then \( T \) is the empty string, and the condition simplifies to checking if the number of '0's in \( X \) and \( Y \) are equal, which is already handled in Case 1.
   - If \( n0 \) exceeds the length of \( S \), it's impossible to find a valid \( T \), so the answer is "No".

4. **Candidate Check**:
   - For valid \( n0 \), the candidate \( T \) is \( S \) truncated to the first \( n0 \) characters. The hashes of the strings generated by \( X \) and \( Y \) using this \( T \) are compared. If they match, the answer is "Yes"; otherwise, "No".

        total_diff = (a - c) * m
        denom = d - b

            h = 0
            for char in pattern:
                h = (h * pow_block + hash_block) % mod_val

1. **Initial Checks**: The solution first checks if the counts of '0's and '1's in \( X \) and \( Y \) are the same. If the counts of '1's are the same but '0's differ, it returns "No" since no \( T \) can balance the lengths.

4. **Candidate Check**: For valid \( n0 \), the solution uses a rolling hash to compare the strings generated by \( X \) and \( Y \) with \( T \) as the first \( n0 \) characters of \( S \). If the hashes match, it returns "Yes"; otherwise, "No".
