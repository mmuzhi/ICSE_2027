To solve this problem, we need to count the number of pairs of distinct numbers (a, b) in a given sequence such that neither number appears adjacent in the original sequence, and it is possible to make both numbers adjacent by swapping any occurrence of a with any occurrence of b any number of times. 

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem requires checking for each pair of distinct numbers (a, b) whether their positions in the sequence meet specific conditions. The key insight is that the four positions (two for a and two for b) must form two disjoint consecutive pairs (i.e., the positions can be rearranged into two pairs of adjacent indices). Additionally, the original positions of a and b should not be adjacent.

2. **Intuition**: For each pair (a, b), we need to verify two main conditions:
   - The two occurrences of a are not adjacent in the original sequence.
   - The two occurrences of b are not adjacent in the original sequence.
   - The four combined positions of a and b can be partitioned into two disjoint consecutive pairs.

3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - **Preprocessing**: For each number, record its two positions and whether they are adjacent.
   - **Consecutive Pairs Processing**: For each consecutive pair in the sequence (i.e., indices (i, i+1)), check if the numbers at these positions form a valid pair (a, b) with distinct values. Track all such valid consecutive pairs for each (a, b) pair, ensuring they are disjoint.
   - **Validation**: For each (a, b) pair that has at least two disjoint consecutive pairs, verify that neither a nor b has adjacent positions in the original sequence.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: The algorithm processes each test case in O(N) time, where N is the number of distinct numbers. For each test case, it scans the sequence once to record positions and flags, and then processes each consecutive pair (O(N) operations). The total complexity across all test cases is O(T*N), where T is the number of test cases, which is efficient given the constraints.

import sys
