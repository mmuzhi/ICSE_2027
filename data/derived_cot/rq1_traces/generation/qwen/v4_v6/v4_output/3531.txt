To solve this problem, we need to determine the minimum total damage Bob will receive before all enemies are defeated, given that each enemy deals damage per second while alive, and Bob can attack one enemy per second dealing a fixed power damage. The key insight is to prioritize enemies based on their damage per unit health ratio to minimize the cumulative damage over time.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem involves scheduling attacks on enemies to minimize the total damage Bob receives. Each enemy has a certain health and damage value. Bob can attack one enemy per second, reducing their health by a fixed power amount. The goal is to find the optimal order of attacks that minimizes the total damage dealt by all enemies over time.

2. **Key Insight**: The optimal strategy involves prioritizing enemies with the highest damage per unit health ratio. This ensures that enemies contributing more damage per unit of health are eliminated first, thus minimizing the total damage over time.

3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - **Calculate Attack Requirements**: For each enemy, compute the number of attacks (ceil(health / power)) needed to defeat them.
   - **Sort Enemies**: Sort the enemies based on their damage per unit health ratio in descending order. This ensures that enemies causing the most damage per attack are targeted first.
   - **Compute Total Damage**: For each enemy in the sorted order, calculate the cumulative time taken to defeat all previous enemies plus the time needed to defeat the current enemy. Multiply this cumulative time by the enemy's damage to get the total damage contributed by that enemy.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: The algorithm involves sorting the enemies, which has a time complexity of O(n log n), where n is the number of enemies. This is efficient given the constraint that n can be up to 10^5.

### Solution Code
```python
class Solution:
    def minDamage(self, power: int, damage: List[int], health: List[int]) -> int:
        n = len(damage)
        p_list = [(h + power - 1) // power for h in health]
        enemies = [(d, p) for d, p in zip(damage, p_list)]
        enemies.sort(key=lambda x: x[0] / x[1], reverse=True)
        total_damage = 0
        cumulative = 0
        for d, p in enemies:
            cumulative += p
            total_damage += d * cumulative
        return total_damage
```

### Explanation
1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem requires minimizing the total damage Bob takes by strategically choosing the order in which he attacks enemies. Each enemy deals damage per second while alive, and Bob can attack one enemy per second.

2. **Key Insight**: By prioritizing enemies with the highest damage per unit health, Bob ensures that the most damaging enemies are eliminated first, reducing the overall damage over time.

3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - **Attack Calculation**: For each enemy, the number of attacks needed to defeat them is calculated using ceiling division (health / power).
   - **Sorting**: Enemies are sorted by their damage per unit health ratio in descending order to prioritize those that deal the most damage per attack.
   - **Damage Calculation**: The total damage is computed by summing the product of each enemy's damage and the cumulative time taken to defeat all enemies up to and including it.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: The sorting step dominates the time complexity, making it O(n log n), which is efficient for the given constraints. The space complexity is O(n) to store the attack counts and sorted list. 

This approach efficiently minimizes the total damage by leveraging a greedy strategy that prioritizes high-damage-per-health enemies first, ensuring optimal performance even for large input sizes.
