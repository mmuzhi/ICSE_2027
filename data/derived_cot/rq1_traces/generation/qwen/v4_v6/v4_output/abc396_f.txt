### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The key observation is that the inversion count for each \( B_k \) can be broken down into three parts:
   - **Unwrapped Inversions**: Pairs where both elements are unwrapped (i.e., \( A_i + k < M \) and \( A_j + k < M \)), and \( A_i > A_j \).
   - **Wrapped Inversions**: Pairs where both elements are wrapped (i.e., \( A_i + k \geq M \) and \( A_j + k \geq M \)), and \( A_i > A_j \).
   - **Mixed Inversions**: Pairs where one element is unwrapped and the other is wrapped, with the unwrapped element appearing before the wrapped element in the original sequence. In these cases, the unwrapped element is always greater than the wrapped element, so they always contribute to the inversion count.
