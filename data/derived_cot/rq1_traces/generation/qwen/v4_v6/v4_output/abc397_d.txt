### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem requires finding integers \(x\) and \(y\) such that the difference of their cubes equals \(N\). The equation \(x^3 - y^3 = N\) can be factored into \((x - y)(x^2 + xy + y^2) = N\). Here, \(d = x - y\) must be a positive integer divisor of \(N\). 

2. **Key Insight**: For each divisor \(d\) of \(N\), we can derive a quadratic equation in terms of \(y\): \(3y^2 + 3dy + (d^2 - \frac{N}{d}) = 0\). The discriminant of this equation must be a perfect square for \(y\) to be an integer. 

3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - Iterate over all possible divisors \(d\) of \(N\) up to the cube root of \(N\) (since \(d^3 \leq N\)).
   - For each divisor \(d\), compute the discriminant \(D = 12 \times \frac{N}{d} - 3d^2\). If \(D\) is a perfect square, solve the quadratic equation to find \(y\).
   - Check if the solution \(y\) is a positive integer. If so, compute \(x = y + d\) and return the pair \((x, y)\).

4. **Complexity Analysis**: The algorithm checks divisors up to \(N^{1/3}\), which for \(N \leq 10^{18}\) is around \(10^6\) operations. Each operation involves basic arithmetic and a square root check, making the solution efficient for large \(N\).
