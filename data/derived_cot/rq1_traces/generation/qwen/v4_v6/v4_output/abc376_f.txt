The challenge is to compute the minimal operations efficiently for each instruction in sequence.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The ring is circular, and both hands start at parts 1 and 2, respectively. Each operation moves one hand to an adjacent part, provided the other hand is not at the destination. The instructions specify which hand must reach a target part. The solution involves calculating the minimal steps for each instruction, considering the current positions of both hands and any blocking conditions.

2. **Key Insight**: For each instruction, the minimal steps depend on the direction (clockwise or counter-clockwise) chosen for the moving hand. The path taken by the moving hand must not include the other hand's initial position unless it is moved out of the way. The minimal steps are the sum of the steps taken by the moving hand and any necessary steps to move the other hand out of the path.

3. **Algorithm Selection**: For each instruction, compute the minimal steps for both possible directions (clockwise and counter-clockwise). For each direction, check if the other hand is in the path. If so, calculate the minimal steps to move the other hand out of the path. The total steps for each direction is the sum of the moving hand's steps and the other hand's steps (if any). The minimal steps across both directions is the answer for the current instruction.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: The algorithm processes each instruction in O(N) time per direction, where N is the number of parts. With Q instructions, the total complexity is O(Q*N), which is feasible given the constraints (N and Q up to 3000).
