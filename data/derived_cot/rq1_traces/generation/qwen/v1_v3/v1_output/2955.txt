=== ANSWER ===

To solve this problem, we need to determine the account balance after purchasing an item where the purchase amount is rounded to the nearest multiple of 10. The rounding follows specific rules: if there are two multiples equally close, the larger one is chosen. 

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The key observation here is that the purchase amount needs to be rounded to the nearest multiple of 10. The rounding rule specifies that if the purchase amount is exactly halfway between two multiples (e.g., 15 is between 10 and 20), the larger multiple should be chosen.
2. **Insight**: The solution involves adjusting the purchase amount by adding 5 and then performing integer division by 10. This adjustment effectively rounds the number to the nearest multiple of 10, with ties rounding up to the larger multiple.
3. **Algorithm Selection**: The algorithm uses arithmetic operations to compute the rounded amount. Specifically, it calculates `(purchaseAmount + 5) // 10 * 10`, which rounds the purchase amount to the nearest multiple of 10, rounding up in case of a tie.
4. **Complexity Analysis**: The operations involved are basic arithmetic operations (addition, integer division, and multiplication), each performed in constant time. Thus, the solution operates in O(1) time with O(1) space complexity.

### Solution Code
```python
class Solution:
    def accountBalanceAfterPurchase(self, purchaseAmount: int) -> int:
        roundedAmount = (purchaseAmount + 5) // 10 * 10
        return 100 - roundedAmount
```

### Explanation
1. **Rounding Logic**: The expression `(purchaseAmount + 5) // 10 * 10` works by first adding 5 to the purchase amount. This adjustment ensures that numbers exactly halfway between two multiples (like 15) will round up. For example:
   - For `purchaseAmount = 9`, `(9 + 5) = 14`, and `14 // 10 = 1`, so the rounded amount is `1 * 10 = 10`.
   - For `purchaseAmount = 15`, `(15 + 5) = 20`, and `20 // 10 = 2`, so the rounded amount is `2 * 10 = 20`.
2. **Balance Calculation**: The rounded amount is subtracted from the initial balance of 100 dollars to get the final account balance. For instance, if the rounded amount is 10, the balance becomes `100 - 10 = 90`.

This approach efficiently handles all edge cases, including amounts that are already multiples of 10 or exactly midway between two multiples, ensuring the correct rounding as specified.
