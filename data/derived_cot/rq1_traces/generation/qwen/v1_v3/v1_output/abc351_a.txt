=== ANSWER ===

To solve this problem, we need to determine the minimum number of runs Team Aoki needs to score in the bottom of the ninth inning to win the game. The key observation here is that Team Aoki's total score after the ninth inning must be strictly greater than Team Takahashi's total score from the top of the ninth inning.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem provides the runs scored by Team Takahashi in each of the nine innings (A1 to A9) and the runs scored by Team Aoki in the first eight innings (B1 to B8). The condition given is that at the end of the top of the ninth inning, Team Takahashi's total score is not less than Team Aoki's total score from the first eight innings.
2. **Key Insight**: To win the game, Team Aoki must score enough runs in the bottom of the ninth inning such that their total score (sum of the first eight innings plus the runs in the ninth inning) exceeds Team Takahashi's total score from the top of the ninth inning.
3. **Algorithm Selection**: The solution involves calculating the total runs for both teams. The minimum runs required for Team Aoki is derived from the inequality: `total_B + x > total_A`, where `total_A` is the sum of Team Takahashi's runs over nine innings, `total_B` is the sum of Team Aoki's runs over eight innings, and `x` is the runs needed in the ninth inning. Solving for `x` gives `x = total_A - total_B + 1`.
4. **Complexity Analysis**: The solution involves simple arithmetic operations (summing two lists and a subtraction) which are efficient with constant time complexity O(1) given the fixed size of the inputs (9 and 8 elements respectively).

### Solution Code
```python
def main():
    A_list = list(map(int, input().split()))
    B_list = list(map(int, input().split()))
    total_A = sum(A_list)
    total_B = sum(B_list)
    result = total_A - total_B + 1
    print(result)

if __name__ == '__main__':
    main()
```

### Explanation
1. **Reading Input**: The code reads two lines of input. The first line contains nine integers representing Team Takahashi's runs in each inning. The second line contains eight integers representing Team Aoki's runs in the first eight innings.
2. **Summing Runs**: The total runs for Team Takahashi (`total_A`) is the sum of all nine innings. The total runs for Team Aoki (`total_B`) is the sum of the first eight innings.
3. **Calculating Minimum Runs**: The minimum runs needed in the ninth inning for Team Aoki to win is computed as `total_A - total_B + 1`. This ensures that Team Aoki's total score after the ninth inning will be strictly greater than Team Takahashi's total score.
4. **Output Result**: The result is printed, which is the minimum number of runs Team Aoki needs to score in the bottom of the ninth inning to win the game.

This approach efficiently leverages basic arithmetic to solve the problem with optimal performance, adhering to the given constraints and conditions.
