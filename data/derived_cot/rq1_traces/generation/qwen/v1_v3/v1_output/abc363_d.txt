### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The key observation is that palindrome numbers can be generated by mirroring the first half of the number. For example, the number 363 is formed by mirroring the first two digits (3 and 6) to create 363. The approach involves determining the number of digits in the N-th palindrome and then constructing it based on its half.

2. **Intuition**: The solution leverages the fact that the count of palindrome numbers with a specific number of digits follows a predictable pattern. For a given digit length `d`, the count of palindromes is calculated based on the first half of the number. For even `d`, the count is 9 * 10^(k-1), where `k` is `d/2`. For odd `d`, it's 9 * 10^k, where `k` is `(d+1)/2`.

3. **Algorithm Selection**: The algorithm iterates through possible digit lengths, subtracting the count of palindromes for each length from `N` until it finds the appropriate length. Once the length is found, the first half of the palindrome is determined, and the full palindrome is constructed by mirroring this half.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: The algorithm efficiently narrows down the digit length in logarithmic time relative to `N`, making it feasible even for very large `N` (up to 10^18). The operations within each iteration are constant time, ensuring optimal performance.
