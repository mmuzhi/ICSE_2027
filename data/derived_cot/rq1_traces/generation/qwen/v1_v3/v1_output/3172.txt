=== ANSWER ===

To solve this problem, we need to compute the difference between the sum of all integers in the range [1, n] that are not divisible by m and the sum of all integers in the same range that are divisible by m. 

### Approach
1. **Calculate Total Sum**: First, compute the sum of all integers from 1 to n using the formula for the sum of an arithmetic series: \( \text{total} = \frac{n \times (n + 1)}{2} \).
2. **Sum of Divisible Numbers**: Determine the sum of all integers in the range [1, n] that are divisible by m. This can be done by identifying the largest integer \( k \) such that \( m \times k \leq n \). The sum of these numbers forms an arithmetic series with the first term \( m \), common difference \( m \), and \( k \) terms. The sum is calculated using the formula \( \text{sum\_divisible} = m \times \frac{k \times (k + 1)}{2} \).
3. **Compute Result**: The result is obtained by subtracting twice the sum of the divisible numbers from the total sum. This is because the difference between the sum of non-divisible numbers and the sum of divisible numbers can be expressed as \( \text{total} - 2 \times \text{sum\_divisible} \).

### Solution Code
```python
class Solution:
    def differenceOfSums(self, n: int, m: int) -> int:
        total = n * (n + 1) // 2
        k = n // m
        sum_divisible = m * (k * (k + 1) // 2)
        return total - 2 * sum_divisible
```

### Explanation
1. **Total Sum Calculation**: The total sum of integers from 1 to n is efficiently calculated using the arithmetic series sum formula, which avoids the need for a loop and operates in constant time.
2. **Divisible Numbers Sum**: The sum of numbers divisible by m is found by first determining how many such numbers exist (k), which is the integer division of n by m. The sum of these numbers is then computed using the arithmetic series sum formula applied to the sequence of multiples of m up to n.
3. **Result Calculation**: The final result is derived by subtracting twice the sum of the divisible numbers from the total sum. This approach efficiently leverages mathematical formulas to avoid explicit iteration, ensuring optimal performance even for the upper constraint limits. 

This method efficiently computes the desired difference by breaking down the problem into manageable mathematical components, ensuring clarity and performance.
