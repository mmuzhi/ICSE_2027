verticalCut of size n - 1, where verticalCut[j] represents the cost to cut along the vertical line j.

In one operation, you can choose any piece of cake that is not yet a 1 x 1 square and perform one of the following cuts:

Cut along a horizontal line i at a cost of horizontalCut[i].
Cut along a vertical line j at a cost of verticalCut[j].

After the cut, the piece of cake is divided into two distinct pieces.
The cost of a cut depends only on the initial cost of the line and does not change.
Return the minimum total cost to cut the entire cake into 1 x 1 pieces.

To solve this problem, we need to determine the minimal cost required to cut an m x n cake into 1x1 pieces using given horizontal and vertical cuts with associated costs. The key observation is that the minimal cost can be achieved by either cutting all vertical cuts first or all horizontal cuts first, and then computing the total cost for both scenarios to choose the cheaper one.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The cake needs to be cut into 1x1 pieces using horizontal and vertical cuts. Each horizontal cut (there are m-1) has a specific cost, and each vertical cut (there are n-1) has a specific cost. The challenge is to find the sequence of cuts that minimizes the total cost.
2. **Insight**: The minimal cost is derived from two strategies:
   - **Strategy 1**: Cut all vertical cuts first. The total cost is the sum of all vertical cuts plus the sum of all horizontal cuts multiplied by the number of columns (n).
   - **Strategy 2**: Cut all horizontal cuts first. The total cost is the sum of all horizontal cuts plus the sum of all vertical cuts multiplied by the number of rows (m).
3. **Algorithm Selection**: The solution involves calculating the total cost for both strategies and selecting the minimum of the two. This approach efficiently narrows down the solution to the optimal sequence without exhaustive search, leveraging the problem's structure.

### Solution Code
```python
class Solution:
    def minimumCost(self, m: int, n: int, horizontalCut: List[int], verticalCut: List[int]) -> int:
        sum_horizontal = sum(horizontalCut)
        sum_vertical = sum(verticalCut)
        return min(sum_vertical + n * sum_horizontal, sum_horizontal + m * sum_vertical)
```

### Explanation
- **Problem Analysis**: The cake is initially one piece. Each cut (horizontal or vertical) splits the cake into more pieces. The cost of each cut depends on the line being cut and is incurred each time the line is used in a cut operation.
- **Insight**: By cutting all vertical cuts first, the cake is divided into columns. Each column then requires all horizontal cuts to be applied, leading to a total cost that is the sum of vertical cuts plus the product of the number of columns and the sum of horizontal cuts. Conversely, cutting all horizontal cuts first divides the cake into rows, each requiring all vertical cuts, resulting in a total cost that is the sum of horizontal cuts plus the product of the number of rows and the sum of vertical cuts.
- **Algorithm Selection**: The solution calculates both total costs and selects the minimum. This approach efficiently narrows down the optimal sequence by considering the two most effective strategies, ensuring the minimal cost without unnecessary computations.
- **Complexity**: The algorithm runs in O(m + n) time, where m and n are the dimensions of the cake, as it involves summing the costs of the cuts once. The space complexity is O(1) as no additional data structures are used beyond simple variables.
