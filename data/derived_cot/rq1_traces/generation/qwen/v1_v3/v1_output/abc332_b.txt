=== ANSWER ===

To solve this problem, we need to simulate a series of operations performed on a glass and a mug over K steps, following specific rules for each operation. The goal is to determine the final amounts of water in both the glass and the mug after all operations are completed.

### Approach
1. **Initialization**: Start with both the glass and the mug empty.
2. **Operations Simulation**: For each of the K operations:
   - **Check if the glass is full**: If the glass contains exactly G milliliters, discard all water from the glass (set it to 0).
   - **If the glass is not full and the mug is empty**: Fill the mug to its capacity M.
   - **Otherwise (glass not full and mug not empty)**: Transfer water from the mug to the glass until the glass is full (G milliliters) or the mug is empty, whichever comes first.
3. **Output the Result**: After performing all K operations, print the final amounts of water in the glass and the mug.

This approach efficiently models each operation step-by-step, ensuring the correct state transitions between the glass and the mug after each operation.

### Solution Code
```python
def main():
    import sys
    data = sys.stdin.read().split()
    K = int(data[0])
    G = int(data[1])
    M = int(data[2])
    
    glass = 0
    mug = 0
    
    for _ in range(K):
        if glass == G:
            glass = 0
        else:
            if mug == 0:
                mug = M
            else:
                transfer = min(mug, G - glass)
                mug -= transfer
                glass += transfer
                
    print(glass, mug)

if __name__ == '__main__':
    main()
```

### Explanation
1. **Initialization**: The glass and mug start with 0 milliliters of water.
2. **Loop through K operations**: For each operation:
   - **Glass Full Check**: If the glass is full (contains G milliliters), it is emptied (set to 0).
   - **Mug Empty Check**: If the glass is not full and the mug is empty, the mug is filled to its maximum capacity M.
   - **Transfer Water**: If neither condition is met, water is transferred from the mug to the glass until the glass is full or the mug is empty. The amount transferred is the minimum of the remaining space in the glass (G - current glass amount) and the current amount in the mug.
3. **Output**: After processing all K operations, the final amounts in the glass and mug are printed.

This approach ensures that each operation is handled according to the given rules, and the state of the glass and mug is updated correctly after each step, leading to the desired result after K operations.
