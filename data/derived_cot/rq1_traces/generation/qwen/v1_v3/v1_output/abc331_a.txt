=== ANSWER ===

To solve this problem, we need to determine the next day in a custom calendar system where each month has a fixed number of days, and each year consists of a fixed number of months. The solution involves checking if the given day is the last day of its month. If it is, the next day will be the first day of the next month. If the next month does not exist, we move to the next year and the first month.

### Approach
1. **Input Reading**: Read the number of months (M) and days per month (D) from the first line of input. Then read the current date (year y, month m, day d) from the second line.
2. **Day Increment Check**: If the current day (d) is less than the maximum days per month (D), simply increment the day by 1 and keep the month and year unchanged.
3. **Month and Year Transition**: If the current day is the last day of the month (d equals D), set the day to 1 and increment the month by 1. If the incremented month exceeds the total months in the year (M), set the month back to 1 and increment the year by 1.
4. **Output**: Print the resulting year, month, and day.

This approach efficiently handles the transition between days, months, and years in the given calendar system by leveraging simple conditional checks and arithmetic operations.

### Solution Code
```python
def main():
    data = input().split()
    M = int(data[0])
    D = int(data[1])
    
    date_data = input().split()
    y = int(date_data[0])
    m = int(date_data[1])
    d = int(date_data[2])
    
    if d < D:
        print(f"{y} {m} {d+1}")
    else:
        d = 1
        m += 1
        if m > M:
            m = 1
            y += 1
        print(f"{y} {m} {d}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
```

### Explanation
1. **Input Handling**: The first line of input is split into two integers, M (number of months) and D (days per month). The second line is split into three integers representing the current date: year (y), month (m), and day (d).
2. **Day Check**: The code checks if the current day (d) is less than the maximum days per month (D). If true, it prints the next day by incrementing d by 1.
3. **Month and Year Transition**: If the current day is the last day of the month (d equals D), the day is reset to 1, and the month is incremented by 1. If the incremented month exceeds the total months in the year (M), the month is reset to 1, and the year is incremented by 1.
4. **Output**: The result is printed in the format "year month day", showing the next day in the custom calendar system.

This solution efficiently handles all edge cases, including transitions from the last day of the month to the first day of the next month and from the last month of the year to the first month of the next year.
