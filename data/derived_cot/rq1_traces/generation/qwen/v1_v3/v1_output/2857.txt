Total distance traveled is 60km.

=== ANSWER ===

To solve this problem, we need to determine the maximum distance a truck can travel given two fuel tanks, considering that fuel is transferred from the additional tank to the main tank whenever the main tank's fuel consumption reaches 5 liters, provided the additional tank has at least 1 liter of fuel. The truck's mileage is 10 km per liter.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The truck starts with a certain amount of fuel in the main tank and the additional tank. The truck consumes fuel in liters, and for every 5 liters consumed from the main tank, 1 liter is transferred from the additional tank to the main tank if the additional tank has enough fuel. The goal is to calculate the total distance the truck can travel before both tanks are empty.

2. **Intuition**: The solution involves simulating the fuel consumption process. The main tank is used first. Whenever the main tank's fuel drops to 5 liters or less, the remaining fuel is used without any further transfers. The key is to handle the transfer of fuel from the additional tank to the main tank immediately after every 5 liters of fuel are consumed from the main tank.

3. **Algorithm Selection**: The simulation approach is chosen because it directly models the fuel consumption and transfer process step-by-step. This approach ensures that all conditions are met correctly, including the immediate transfer of fuel after every 5 liters consumed.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: The algorithm processes each liter of fuel in the main tank once. Given the constraints (mainTank and additionalTank are up to 100 liters), the time complexity is O(n), where n is the total fuel consumed, which is efficient for the given constraints.

### Solution Code
```python
class Solution:
    def distanceTraveled(self, mainTank: int, additionalTank: int) -> int:
        total_fuel = 0
        while mainTank > 0:
            if mainTank >= 5 and additionalTank > 0:
                use = 5
            else:
                use = mainTank
            mainTank -= use
            total_fuel += use
            if use == 5 and additionalTank > 0:
                additionalTank -= 1
                mainTank += 1
        return total_fuel * 10
```

### Explanation
1. **Initialization**: The variable `total_fuel` is initialized to 0 to keep track of the total fuel consumed.
2. **Loop Until Main Tank is Empty**: The loop continues as long as there is fuel in the main tank.
3. **Fuel Consumption**: In each iteration, the algorithm checks if the main tank has at least 5 liters and the additional tank has fuel. If true, it consumes 5 liters from the main tank; otherwise, it consumes the remaining fuel in the main tank.
4. **Transfer Fuel**: After consuming 5 liters from the main tank, if the additional tank has fuel, 1 liter is transferred from the additional tank to the main tank.
5. **Update Total Fuel**: The consumed fuel is added to `total_fuel` in each iteration.
6. **Return Distance**: The total distance is calculated by multiplying the total fuel consumed by 10 (since the truck's mileage is 10 km per liter).

This approach efficiently simulates the fuel consumption and transfer process, ensuring accurate calculation of the maximum distance the truck can travel.
