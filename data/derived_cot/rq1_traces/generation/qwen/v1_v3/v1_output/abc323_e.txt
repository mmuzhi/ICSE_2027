### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The key observation is that for the first song to be playing at (X + 0.5) seconds, the start time of the first song must be such that it hasn't finished by (X + 0.5) seconds. This translates to the sum of the durations of the previous songs (if any) being in the range [L, R], where L is the smallest integer greater than (X + 0.5 - T₁) and R is X. 

2. **Dynamic Programming (DP) Setup**: We use a DP array where `dp[s]` represents the probability that the sum of the durations of a sequence of songs is `s`. The DP array is initialized with `dp[0] = 1` (the empty sequence has probability 1).

3. **DP Array Population**: For each second `s` from 1 to X, compute `dp[s]` by summing the probabilities of all sequences that can reach `s` by adding one song's duration. Each song's duration is considered with a probability of `1/N`.

4. **Probability Calculation**: After populating the DP array, sum the probabilities of all sequences where the sum of durations is within [L, R]. Multiply this sum by `1/N` (the probability of selecting the first song next) to get the final result.

5. **Modulo Handling**: Since the result needs to be reported modulo 998244353, all operations, including division (using modular inverses), are performed under this modulus.
