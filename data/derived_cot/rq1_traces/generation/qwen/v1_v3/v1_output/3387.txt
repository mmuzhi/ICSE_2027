To solve this problem, we need to determine the minimum number of operations required to adjust the elements of an array such that the median of the resulting array equals a given integer \( k \). Each operation allows increasing or decreasing an element by 1. The median is defined as the middle element in the sorted array; if the array length is even, the larger of the two middle elements is chosen.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The median of a sorted array is the element at the middle index (0-based). For the median to be \( k \), the array must be adjusted such that after sorting, the element at the middle index is \( k \). This requires ensuring that there are at most \( \text{mid\_index} \) elements less than \( k \) and at most \( n - \text{mid\_index} - 1 \) elements greater than \( k \), where \( \text{mid\_index} = \lfloor n/2 \rfloor \).

2. **Intuition**: The solution involves categorizing elements into those less than \( k \), those greater than \( k \), and those equal to \( k \). The elements less than \( k \) that exceed the allowed count (i.e., more than \( \text{mid\_index} \)) must be increased to \( k \). Similarly, elements greater than \( k \) that exceed the allowed count (i.e., more than \( n - \text{mid\_index} - 1 \)) must be decreased to \( k \).

3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - **Separate Elements**: Split the array into elements less than \( k \) and elements greater than \( k \).
   - **Calculate Deficits**: Compute how many elements from each category need to be adjusted to meet the median condition.
   - **Optimal Adjustments**: For the elements less than \( k \), adjust the largest ones first (to minimize operations). For elements greater than \( k \), adjust the smallest ones first (to minimize operations). The total operations are the sum of the differences between these elements and \( k \).

4. **Complexity Analysis**: The algorithm involves sorting two lists (elements less than \( k \) and elements greater than \( k \)), which has a time complexity of \( O(n \log n) \). Given the constraints \( n \leq 2 \times 10^5 \), this is efficient.
