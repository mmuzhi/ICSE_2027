=== ANSWER ===

To solve this problem, we need to minimize the total cost of purchasing N items using up to M coupons, where each coupon can be applied only once to an item whose regular price is at least the coupon's specified minimum price. The goal is to maximize the total discount applied, thereby minimizing the total cost.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem involves matching coupons to items such that each coupon is used at most once, and each item can receive at most one coupon. The discount from a coupon is applied only if the item's price is at least the coupon's minimum price. The challenge is to maximize the total discount, which directly minimizes the total cost.

2. **Intuition**: The optimal strategy is to use the highest-value coupons (largest discounts) first on the most eligible items. By sorting items by price and coupons by their minimum price, we can efficiently match coupons to items in a way that maximizes the total discount.

3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - **Sorting**: Sort the items by their prices in ascending order and the coupons by their minimum prices in ascending order.
   - **Greedy Matching**: Use a max-heap (priority queue) to keep track of available coupons. For each item, add all coupons that can be applied (i.e., coupons with minimum price ≤ item's price) to the heap. Then, apply the best available coupon (highest discount) to the current item.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: 
   - Sorting the items and coupons takes O(N log N) and O(M log M) time, respectively.
   - Processing each item and coupon involves heap operations (insertions and extractions) which take O(log M) time per operation. In the worst case, all coupons might be processed, leading to O((N + M) log M) time.
   - The overall complexity is dominated by sorting and heap operations, making it efficient for large inputs (up to 200,000 elements).

```python
    ans = total_sum - total_discount
    print(ans)

if __name__ == '__main__':
    main()
```

### Explanation
1. **Reading Input**: The input is read in one go for efficiency, especially given the constraints. The first two values are the number of items (N) and coupons (M). The next N values are the item prices, followed by M values for the minimum prices (L_i) and discounts (D_i) of the coupons.

2. **Sorting**: The item prices are sorted in ascending order to process cheaper items first. Coupons are sorted by their minimum price to ensure we only consider relevant coupons for each item.

3. **Greedy Matching with Heap**: A max-heap (simulated using negative values) is used to keep track of available coupons. For each item, all coupons with a minimum price less than or equal to the item's price are added to the heap. The best coupon (highest discount) is then applied to the item, reducing the total cost by the discount amount.

4. **Output**: The total cost is calculated by subtracting the total discount from the sum of all item prices and printed.

This approach efficiently maximizes the total discount by leveraging sorting and a greedy strategy with a heap, ensuring optimal performance even for large input sizes.
