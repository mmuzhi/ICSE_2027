The key observation is that each defeat requires energy, and each mark (operation 2) provides energy by adding the marked enemy's energy. The optimal strategy involves using the smallest enemy energies for defeats to minimize the energy cost per defeat and marking the largest enemies to maximize the energy available for more defeats.

### Approach
1. **Identify the smallest enemy energy (a0)**: This is crucial because defeats on smaller enemies consume less energy, allowing more defeats.
2. **Check initial energy**: If the initial energy is less than the smallest enemy energy, no defeats can be performed, so return 0.
3. **Calculate total available energy**: The total energy available is the sum of the initial energy and all enemy energies minus the smallest enemy energy. This adjustment accounts for leaving at least one smallest enemy unmarked to continue defeats.
4. **Compute maximum defeats**: The maximum number of defeats is the floor division of the total available energy by the smallest enemy energy, as each defeat costs at least the smallest enemy energy.

### Solution Code

class Solution:
    def maximumPoints(self, enemyEnergies: List[int], currentEnergy: int) -> int:
        a0 = min(enemyEnergies)
        if currentEnergy < a0:

        total_energy = currentEnergy + sum(enemyEnergies) - a0

### Explanation
1. **Finding the smallest enemy energy (a0)**: The smallest energy value among all enemies determines the minimal energy required for each defeat.
2. **Initial energy check**: If the initial energy is insufficient to defeat the smallest enemy, no operations can be performed, hence return 0.
3. **Total energy calculation**: The sum of the initial energy and all enemy energies minus the smallest enemy energy gives the net energy available for defeats. This adjustment ensures that at least one smallest enemy remains unmarked for continued defeats.
4. **Maximizing defeats**: The total available energy divided by the smallest enemy energy gives the maximum number of defeats possible, as each defeat consumes at least this much energy. The floor division ensures we account for integer defeats only.

This approach efficiently leverages the smallest enemy for minimizing energy consumption per defeat and marks larger enemies to maximize available energy, ensuring optimal performance even for large input sizes.
