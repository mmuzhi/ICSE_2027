=== ANSWER ===

To solve this problem, we need to determine the final score of the last person (person N) in a game scenario where each game involves two players, and the winner's score increases by 1 while the loser's score decreases by 1. The key observation here is that the total sum of all players' scores must be zero because each game results in a net change of zero (one player gains 1 point, and the other loses 1 point). 

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem involves N people playing several one-on-one games. The initial scores of all players are zero. The final scores of the first N-1 players are given, and we need to find the score of the last player. The critical insight is that the sum of all players' scores must be zero due to the nature of the games (each game contributes a net zero change to the total score).
2. **Intuition**: Since the total sum of all scores must be zero, the score of the last player can be derived by taking the negative of the sum of the given scores of the first N-1 players.
3. **Algorithm Selection**: The algorithm involves reading the given scores of the first N-1 players, summing them up, and then computing the last player's score as the negative of this sum.
4. **Complexity Analysis**: The solution involves a single pass through the list of scores to compute the sum, resulting in a time complexity of O(N), where N is the number of players. The space complexity is O(1) as we only store the sum and the input scores.

### Solution Code
```python
n = int(input().strip())
a_list = list(map(int, input().split()))
total = sum(a_list)
print(-total)
```

### Explanation
1. **Reading Input**: The first line of input is the number of players, N. The second line contains the scores of the first N-1 players.
2. **Sum Calculation**: The sum of the given scores (first N-1 players) is calculated. According to the problem's invariant, the total sum of all players' scores must be zero. Therefore, the score of the last player is the negative of this sum.
3. **Output Result**: The computed score for the last player is printed.

This approach efficiently leverages the invariant that the total sum of all players' scores is zero, allowing us to determine the last player's score with minimal computation. The solution is both optimal and straightforward, adhering to the problem constraints and requirements.
