### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem involves tracking the position of a black ball in a sequence of N balls after K operations. The challenge is to compute the expected position of the black ball after K operations, considering the large constraints (N up to 998244353 and K up to 10^5).

2. **Key Insight**: The position of the black ball can be modeled using a linear recurrence relation. The expected value after each operation depends on the previous expected value and the probabilities of the ball moving to different positions. 3. **Mathematical Formulation**: The recurrence relation for the expected value \( F_k \) (0-indexed) after k operations is:

   where \( a = \frac{N-2}{N} \) and \( b = \frac{N-1}{N} \). Here, \( a \) is the probability of the ball staying in the same position, and \( b \) is the expected value when the ball moves to a different position.

4. **Modular Arithmetic**: Since the result needs to be reported modulo 998244353, we use modular inverses to handle divisions. The modular inverse of a number \( x \) is computed as \( x^{998244351} \mod 998244353 \).
