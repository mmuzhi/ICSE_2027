To solve this problem, we need to determine whether we can plant at least `n` flowers in a given flowerbed without violating the rule that no two adjacent flowers can be planted. The solution involves checking each spot in the flowerbed to see if it can accommodate a flower based on its neighboring spots.

### Approach
1. **Padding the Flowerbed**: We start by padding the flowerbed with zeros at both ends. This simplifies boundary checks by ensuring that edge spots have virtual neighbors (zeros) to consider.
2. **Iterating Through the Flowerbed**: For each spot in the flowerbed (excluding the padded zeros), we check if the current spot and its immediate neighbors are all zeros. If they are, we plant a flower (change the current spot to 1) and decrement the count of flowers needed (`n`).
3. **Checking Completion**: After processing all spots, if the remaining count of flowers needed (`n`) is zero or less, we return `True`; otherwise, we return `False`.

This approach efficiently checks each spot in a left-to-right manner, ensuring that no two adjacent flowers are planted. The padding step helps in handling edge cases uniformly without additional boundary checks.
