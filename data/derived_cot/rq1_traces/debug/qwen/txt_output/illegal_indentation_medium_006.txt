To solve this problem, we need to find all words in `words1` that are "supersets" of every word in `words2` in terms of character frequency. A word from `words1` must contain at least the characters found in each word of `words2` with at least the same or higher frequency.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The task is to determine which words in `words1` include all the characters from any word in `words2` with sufficient frequency. For each word in `words2`, we need to combine their character frequencies into a single counter that represents the maximum frequency of each character across all words in `words2`.
2. **Intuition**: By combining the character frequencies of all words in `words2` into a single counter (using the union operation), we can then check each word in `words1` to see if its character frequencies meet or exceed those in the combined counter.
3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - **Combining Character Frequencies**: Use the `reduce` function with a lambda function to combine all word counters from `words2` into one counter that takes the maximum frequency for each character.
   - **Checking Superset Condition**: For each word in `words1`, convert it into a counter and check if it meets or exceeds the frequencies in the combined counter from `words2`.
4. **Complexity Analysis**: The solution involves processing each word in `words2` once to build the combined counter (O(n * m) where n is the number of words in `words2` and m is the average length of those words). Then, for each word in `words1`, we perform a similar operation (O(k * l) where k is the number of words in `words1` and l is their average length). The overall complexity is linear with respect to the total number of characters in both lists.

```python
from collections import Counter
from functools import reduce
```
