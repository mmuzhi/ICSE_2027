### Approach
1. **Sorting the Stones**: The first step is to sort the list of stones in ascending order. This allows us to systematically analyze the positions of the stones and compute the required moves efficiently.
2. **Calculating Maximum Moves**: The maximum number of moves is determined by two scenarios:
   - **Moving the Second Last Stone**: The difference between the second last stone and the first stone minus the length of the stone list plus 2.
   - **Moving the Last Stone**: The difference between the last stone and the second stone minus the length of the stone list plus 2.
   The maximum of these two values gives the upper bound on the number of moves.
3. **Handling Special Cases**: If either of the above scenarios results in zero, it means the stones are already in a configuration that requires minimal moves. In such cases, the minimum moves are constrained to either 2 or the calculated maximum moves.
4. **Calculating Minimum Moves**: For cases where the maximum moves are not zero, we iterate through the sorted stones to find the longest contiguous segment of stones that can be left in place without any gaps. The minimum moves required is then the total number of stones minus the length of this contiguous segment.

class Solution:
    def numMovesStonesII(self, stones: List[int]) -> List[int]:
