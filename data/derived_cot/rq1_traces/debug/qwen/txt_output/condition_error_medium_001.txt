1. **Problem Analysis**: The problem requires checking if the array can be divided into contiguous subsequences where each subsequence is either two equal numbers (e.g., [a, a]) or three consecutive numbers (e.g., [a, a+1, a+2]). The solution must efficiently verify all possible partitions.

3. **Base Cases**:

   - For arrays of length 2 or 3, directly check if they meet the partition criteria.

The validity of these partitions depends on the results of previous partitions stored in the `dp` array.
5. **Result Extraction**: The result is found in `dp[n]`, which indicates whether the entire array can be partitioned according to the given rules.
