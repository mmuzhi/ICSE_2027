### Approach
1. **Validation of Input Matrix**: 
   - **Symmetry Check**: Ensure that `lcp[i][j]` equals `lcp[j][i]` for all `i` and `j`. If not, the matrix is invalid.
   - **Diagonal Check**: Verify that the diagonal elements `lcp[i][i]` are exactly `n - i`, as the common prefix of a string with itself should be the entire substring starting at `i`.
   - **Boundary Check**: Ensure that `lcp[i][j]` does not exceed the minimum of the remaining lengths of the substrings starting at `i` and `j`.

2. **Pattern Construction**:
   - **Grouping Indices**: Assign indices to groups where all indices in a group must have the same character. This is based on the condition that if `lcp[i][j] > 0`, then the first character of `s[i:]` and `s[j:]` must be the same.
   - **Pattern Assignment**: Assign unique identifiers to each group to ensure characters are distinct across different groups.

3. **Pattern Verification**:
   - **LCP Matrix Reconstruction**: Compute the LCP matrix from the constructed pattern and verify it matches the input matrix.
   - **Character Limit Check**: Ensure the number of distinct characters in the pattern does not exceed 26 (the number of lowercase English letters).
