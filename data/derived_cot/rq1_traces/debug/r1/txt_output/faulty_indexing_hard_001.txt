=== RAW ANSWER ===
To solve this problem, we need to determine the maximum minimum power achievable for any city after adding at most `k` additional power to the existing stations. The cities are arranged in a line, and each station at position `i` covers cities within the range `[i - r, i + r]`. 

### Approach
1. **Binary Search Setup**: We use binary search to determine the maximum feasible minimum power. The search range starts from the minimum power in the existing stations (`min(stations)`) to the total power including the additional `k` units (`sum(stations) + k`).
2. **Feasibility Check (check function)**: For each candidate minimum power `target` during the binary search, we check if it's possible to achieve this target for all cities by adding at most `k` units of power. 
   - **Sliding Window**: We maintain a sliding window to efficiently compute the current power for each city. The window covers stations from `max(0, i - r)` to `min(n-1, i + r)` for city `i`.
   - **Greedy Addition**: If the current city's power is below the target, we add the required difference (`target - power`) at the farthest station within the current window (i.e., `min(i + r, n - 1)`) to maximize coverage for subsequent cities.
   - **Budget Management**: We ensure the total additions do not exceed `k`. If at any point the remaining budget is insufficient to meet the target, we conclude the candidate is infeasible.
3. **Reverting Changes**: After each feasibility check, we revert any modifications made to the stations array to ensure it remains unchanged for subsequent checks.

3. **Result Determination**: After the binary search completes, the highest feasible target (`end` or `start`) is returned as the maximum achievable minimum power.
