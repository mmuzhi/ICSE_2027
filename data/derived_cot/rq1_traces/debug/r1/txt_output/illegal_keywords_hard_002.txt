To solve this problem, we need to find the maximum performance of a team of at most `k` engineers. The performance of a team is defined as the sum of their speeds multiplied by the minimum efficiency among them. 

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The task involves selecting up to `k` engineers such that the product of the minimum efficiency in the team and the sum of their speeds is maximized. The challenge is to efficiently explore all possible teams without resorting to a brute-force approach, which would be computationally expensive.

2. **Key Insight**: By sorting the engineers in descending order of efficiency, we can ensure that when we process each engineer, the current engineer's efficiency is the minimum in any team formed with subsequent engineers. This allows us to focus on maximizing the sum of speeds for the team while the current efficiency is fixed.

3. **Algorithm Selection**: 
   - **Sorting**: Engineers are sorted by their efficiency in descending order. This ensures that each engineer considered can potentially be the one with the minimum efficiency in their team.
   - **Heap Usage**: A min-heap is used to keep track of the speeds of the selected engineers. This helps in efficiently managing the top `k` speeds. If the number of engineers exceeds `k`, the smallest speed is removed from the heap to maintain the team size constraint.
   - **Sum Tracking**: The sum of the speeds in the heap is dynamically updated. For each engineer, after adjusting the heap, the performance (current efficiency multiplied by the sum of speeds) is calculated and the maximum performance is tracked.

4. **Complexity Analysis**: 
   - **Sorting**: Sorting the engineers takes O(n log n) time.
   - **Heap Operations**: Each insertion and extraction from the heap takes O(log k) time. Since these operations are performed for each of the `n` engineers, the total time complexity is O(n log k).
   - **Overall Complexity**: The combined time complexity is O(n log n + n log k), which simplifies to O(n log n) since log n dominates log k for typical values of k.

import heapq
from typing import List

class Solution:
    def maxPerformance(self, n: int, speed: List[int], efficiency: List[int], k: int) -> int:
        MOD = 10**9 + 7
        top_k_speeds = []

3. **Processing Engineers**: For each engineer:

   - If the heap size exceeds `k`, the smallest speed is removed from the heap and subtracted from the sum.
   - The current performance (product of current efficiency and the sum of speeds) is calculated and compared to update the maximum performance (`res`).
4. **Result**: The maximum performance modulo \(10^9 + 7\) is returned as the result.

This approach efficiently maximizes team performance by leveraging sorting and heap operations to dynamically manage the best team configurations while adhering to the constraint of selecting at most `k` engineers.

                top_k_sum -= heapq.heappop(top_k_speeds)
                
            res = max(res, e * top_k_sum)
            
        return res % MOD
